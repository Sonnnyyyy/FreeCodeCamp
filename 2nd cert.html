Beef kare-kare Ingredients and Instructions

Description
Beef kare kare is a mouthwatering Filipino stew that's as rich in history as it is in flavor. Picture tender chunks of beef, slowly simmered in a thick, peanut-based sauce that's been infused with the aroma of aromatic spices and vegetables. The result is a sumptuous dish that's both savory and satisfying, with a texture that's velvety smooth and a taste that's simply unforgettable. One bite of this delightful concoction and you'll be transported to the heart of the Philippines, where the locals have been perfecting the art of kare kare for generations. So why wait? Grab a spoon, dig in, and discover why this beloved dish has become a national treasure.
 Ingredients
1 kg beef sirloin or stewing beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
1 bunch bok choy, cut into bite-sized pieces
1 kg beef tripe, cut into bite-sized pieces
1 tbsp annatto powder or paprika
2 eggplants, cut into cubes
1 lb green beans, trimmed
5 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup sunflower butter
1/2 cup peanut butter
2 tbsp cooking oil
1 onion, chopped
1/4 cup vinegar
2 tbsp soy sauce
4 cups water
Instructions
1. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot. Sauté garlic and onion until softened.
2. Add the beef tripe and beef sirloin/stewing beef. Sauté until browned on all sides.
3. In a separate bowl, mix together the peanut butter, water, vinegar, soy sauce, and annatto powder/paprika. Add the mixture to the pot and stir well.
4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 1-2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
5. Add the green beans, eggplants, and bok choy to the pot and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
6.Serve the beef kare-kare hot with steamed rice.
